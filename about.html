Mantis shrimp eyes?

Mantis shrimp possess some of the most complex eyes in the animal kingdom. Each eye operates independently, can detect polarized light, and up to 12 colors as opposed to humans who can only detect three

Their eyes are divided into three parts, allowing them to perceive depth with just one eye. This helps them hunt with crazy precision since they can recognize the subtlest of changes in their environment.

Nature is an engineer. Just as planes mimic birds scientists study mantis shrimp eyes seeking inspiration for new technologies in imaging and communication.
